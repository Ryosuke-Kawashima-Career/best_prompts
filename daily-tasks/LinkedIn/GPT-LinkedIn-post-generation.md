# LinkedIn Post Generator

## Objective

Generate a **highly engaging, insightful LinkedIn post** based on the user's experience.

The post should:

- Appeal to **technophiles, entrepreneurs, and business professionals**
- Be **thought-provoking, well-structured, and original**
- Spark **meaningful discussion and quality connections**

---

## Input

### 1. Experience *(Required)*

Describe a real experience — an event, meeting, project, failure, travel, or research moment. Include specific context: who was involved, where it happened, what unfolded.

```text
[Write your experience here]
```

### 2. Key Insight(s) *(Required)*

What did you learn or realize from this experience? Write 1–3 insights, each on a new line.

```text
[Write your key insights here]
```

### 3. Output Language *(Required)*

Select the language for the generated post:

- `English` — International audience, global professional tone
- `Japanese` — Native Japanese phrasing; avoid katakana loanwords unless they are industry-standard (e.g. AI, DX); use polite but confident keigo level appropriate for LinkedIn (です・ます調)

```text
[ ] English
[ ] Japanese
```

### 4. Additional Context *(Optional)*

Anything worth mentioning to enrich the post:

- People to acknowledge
- Broader themes (AI, leadership, market trends, society)
- Personal reflection or future direction

```text
[Write any optional context here]
```

---

## Writing Instructions

Follow this post structure **strictly and in order**:

### 1. Title

- Short, memorable, slightly provocative
- Captures the core insight — not just the event
- Should make someone stop scrolling

### 2. Hook *(1–3 lines)*

- Open with a concrete, vivid moment from the experience
- Avoid generic openers — place the reader *inside* the scene
- Create immediate tension or curiosity

### 3. Observation

- What surprised you?
- What felt counterintuitive or unexpected?
- Keep it grounded in the specific experience

### 4. Insight *(Generalization)*

- Extract a broader lesson applicable beyond the anecdote
- Connect it to one or more of: business strategy, AI/technology, leadership, or entrepreneurship
- This is the intellectual core of the post — make it memorable

### 5. Application

- Why does this insight matter to your audience?
- How can a reader concretely apply or act on it?
- Avoid vague advice — be specific and actionable

### 6. Forward-Looking Statement

- What will you do differently going forward?
- What belief did this reinforce or challenge?
- End with conviction, not hedging

### 7. Networking Close

- Invite the audience to share their perspective or experience
- Keep it natural and conversational — not transactional
- One question or open-ended prompt is enough

## Style Guidelines

### Universal (both languages)

| Dimension | Guidance |
| --------- | -------- |
| **Tone** | Professional, reflective, analytical — confident without being arrogant |
| **Voice** | First person, direct, clear |
| **Length** | 150–300 words for the body (not counting title and hashtags) |
| **Avoid** | Vague superlatives, filler openers, emotional clichés |
| **Prefer** | Concrete examples, logical structure, precise language, one strong analogy if appropriate |

### English-specific

| Dimension | Guidance |
| --------- | -------- |
| **Register** | International professional English — accessible to non-native readers |
| **Avoid** | "I'm excited to share", "humbled", "game-changer" unless used ironically |
| **Hashtags** | Use English hashtags only (e.g. `#AIStrategy`, `#Leadership`) |

### Japanese-specific

| Dimension | Guidance |
| --------- | -------- |
| **Register** | です・ます調 — polite yet direct; avoid overly formal 敬語 or overly casual 口語 |
| **Sentence length** | Prefer shorter sentences; break complex ideas across lines for readability |
| **Loanwords** | Use katakana only for established industry terms (AI、DX、リーダーシップ); translate or explain others |
| **Avoid** | Vague humility phrases (「微力ながら」「おかげさまで」); keep confidence appropriate for LinkedIn |
| **Hashtags** | Mix Japanese and English hashtags (e.g. `#AI活用` `#リーダーシップ` `#キャリア` + English equivalents) |

---

## Output Format

Produce the following, clearly separated, **in the selected language**:

1. **Title** — One line, bold
2. **Post Body** — Well-spaced paragraphs (one idea per block)
3. **Hashtags** — 5 to 8 relevant, targeted hashtags (see language-specific rules above)

> If Japanese is selected, the entire output — title, body, and hashtags — must be in Japanese, except for hashtags where English tags may be mixed in.

---

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, verify the post:

- [ ] Delivers **one clear, memorable takeaway**
- [ ] Reads as **thought leadership**, not a personal diary
- [ ] Combines **personal story + transferable business insight**
- [ ] Is **globally understandable** (no jargon without explanation)
- [ ] Has a **strong opening line** that earns the rest of the read
- [ ] Ends with a **genuine invitation**, not a call-to-action gimmick
